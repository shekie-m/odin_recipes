Potato wedges recipe

The evolution of french fries gave birth to potato wedges. If you are tired of having your fries plain and boring, then try this instead.

Let's get cooking, shall we?

Meal preparation description

  • Prep time:10mins
  • Cook time:30mins
  • Total time:35mins
  • Servings:4
  • Yield:16 potato wedges

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized irish potatoes
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon groung paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon parsley flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch

Procedure

  1. Peel each potato (optional) and rinse to wash off dirt.
  2. Cut each potato in half lengthwise. Cut each half in half lengthwise, and then cut each quarter in half lenthwise; to obtain 16 wedges
  3. Boil the dices potatoes for 8 minutes and not more to prevent the potatoes from breaking.
  4. Drain any excess water. Place the potatoes on a tray to cool for like 10 minutes.
  5. Into a bowl, add the spices and mix them well until they are well incorporated. Add corn starch and some little water to turn the mixture into a paste.
  6. Add the potatoes into the mixture and mix well gently to avoid breaking the potatoes.
  7. Dip the potatoes in dry flour with salt to dry them.
  8. In preheated oil, deep fry the potatoes until golden brown. Let the mixture drain in paper towels.

Enjoy your potato wedges with some sauce and some juice.
